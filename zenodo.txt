Temporal Equivalence Principle: EFT Mapping and CMB Acoustic Peak Preservation

General Relativity is extensively validated in the screened Solar System, but cosmological tensions—including the Hubble discrepancy—motivate scale-dependent departures from the isochrony axiom. The Temporal Equivalence Principle (TEP) introduces a dynamical proper-time field, screened at densities ρ > 20 g/cm³. This work maps the TEP bi-metric framework (conformal factor A(φ) and disformal deformation B(φ)) into the Bellini-Sawicki Effective Field Theory of Dark Energy, integrates the resulting α_M, α_B, α_K, α_T functions into hi_class, and runs Cobaya MCMC against Planck 2018 high-ℓ, low-ℓ, and lensing likelihoods.

Key results: exact EFT mapping of TEP; radiation-domination freezing preserves acoustic peaks (|ΔC_ℓ/C_ℓ| < 0.02%); CMB H₀ posterior remains Planck-consistent while late-time environmental dynamics can address the local H₀ tension.
